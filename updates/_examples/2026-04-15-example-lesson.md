We hosted our second stakeholder workshop last week, and we want to
share what worked and what didn't, in case it helps other pilots
planning similar engagement activities.

## What worked

- **Mixing roles in each table.** We seated technical staff next to
  policy people next to civic association representatives, and the
  conversations were noticeably richer than at our first workshop where
  people had naturally clustered by background.
- **A concrete artefact to react to.** Showing a working prototype of
  one of our planned services produced ten times more useful feedback
  than the abstract "what would you like to see" question we had used
  at our first workshop.
- **Decent food.** Sounds trivial, but a proper lunch led people to
  stay for the afternoon session instead of drifting off.

## What did not work

- **Three hours was too long.** Energy dropped sharply after two hours.
  Next time we will aim for two hours with a clear closing time.
- **The agenda was too dense.** We tried to cover four topics and only
  had time to do two of them well. Less is more.
- **We did not ask people to come prepared.** Sending a one-page brief
  in advance would have shortened the introductions and got us to the
  substantive discussion faster.

## What we are taking forward

Our third workshop, planned for April, will be two hours, focused on a
single topic, with a one-page brief sent a week in advance. We will
report back on whether this works.

If any other pilot is planning stakeholder engagement and would like to
exchange notes, we would be happy to compare experiences.
